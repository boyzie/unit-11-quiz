The use of Virtual Reality (VR) technology to help people with disabilities "walk through" a potential new home or simulate care options is directly grounded in duties under which piece of legislation?
A. The Data Protection Act 2018 (GDPR).
B. The Human Rights Act (1998), Article 10 (Freedom of Expression).
C. The Mental Capacity Act (2005).
D. The Care Act (2014).

‚úÖ Correct Answer: C

This approach is grounded in the Mental Capacity Act 2005, which requires practitioners to maximise an individual's participation in decisions and take all practical steps to help them make their own choices before concluding they lack capacity. Using immersive visual experiences allows people who struggle with abstract explanations to better understand their options and express informed preferences.
